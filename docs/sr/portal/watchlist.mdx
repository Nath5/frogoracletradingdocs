# Watchlist

The Watchlist feature allows you to track multiple stocks in one convenient location, making it easy to monitor your favorite tickers and quickly switch between them for analysis.

## Accessing the Watchlist

1. Click the watchlist icon ( horizontal lines ) from the chart toolbar

![image](_media/ui/chart_toolbar.png ':size=600')

The watchlist panel will open on the right of the screen.

![image](_media/ui/watchlist.png ':size=600')

## Adding Tickers to Your Watchlist

There are multiple ways to add tickers to your watchlist:

### From the Watchlist Panel

1. Open the Watchlist panel from the sidebar
2. Click the "Import / Add Tickers" button
3. Enter ticker symbols in one of the supported formats:
   - One per line (e.g., AAPL, GOOGL, MSFT)
   - Comma-separated (e.g., AAPL, GOOGL, MSFT)
   - Upload a CSV or TXT file
4. Click "Import" to add them to your watchlist

![image](_media/ui/watchlist_import.png ':size=600')

## Managing Your Watchlist


### Removing Tickers

To remove a ticker from your watchlist:

1. Hover over the ticker in the watchlist
2. Click the remove icon (trashcan) that appears
3. The ticker will be immediately removed from your list

### Organizing Your List

Tickers in your watchlist can be reordered by:

1. Click and hold on a ticker drag icon
2. Drag it to your desired position
3. Release to drop it in the new location

## Loading Tickers from Watchlist

To quickly load a ticker from your watchlist onto the chart:

1. Open the Watchlist panel
2. Click on any ticker in your list
3. The chart will automatically load the selected ticker

This provides a fast way to switch between your tracked stocks without using the ticker search.

## Integration with Charts

The Watchlist feature is designed to enhance your trading workflow:

- **Quick Access**: Switch between tickers instantly without typing
- **Persistent Storage**: Your watchlist is saved and syncs across your devices

## Best Practices

### Building Your Watchlist

- **Sector Focus**: Group related stocks to monitor sector trends
- **Key Levels**: Add stocks that are approaching important support/resistance levels
- **Event Tracking**: Include stocks with upcoming earnings or news events
- **Favorites**: Keep your most-traded stocks readily accessible
